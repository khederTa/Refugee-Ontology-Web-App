INTRODUCTION

The past decade has seen a significant increase in global refugee movements, largely due to conflicts in the Middle East. This has resulted in a refugee crisis, particularly within the European Union, causing political and social upheaval. This paper aims to develop an ontology to improve the management of refugee-related issues, focusing on the concept of home return. Despite extensive academic literature on refugees, practical application of this knowledge in governance remains challenging. The proposed ontology aims to bridge this gap, providing a structured approach to understanding and applying concepts from refugee studies. The methodology offers logical rigor and facilitates knowledge organization and semantic search. The long-term goals include enabling policy-makers to understand and apply concepts from the literature, and to upload real data to test relations between various concepts and measure program impact. The paper focuses on the complex issue of refugee home return, aiming to formally model this elusive field of knowledge.
